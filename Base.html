Your Voice Has Been Leaking Your Mental State Your Entire Life.

This isn't science fiction — it's neuroscience.

When you're stressed, anxious, or depressed, your autonomic nervous system alters how your vocal cords vibrate. Micro-changes in pitch, jitter, shimmer, and spectral energy — signals you can't control, can't fake, and can't suppress.

Researchers have known this for decades. What's new is a 244M-parameter model trained on 35,000 patients that can read these signals from 25 seconds of natural speech. In clinical studies, AI detected stress markers 6.7 minutes before athletes self-reported — with 84.7% accuracy.

Unmanaged stress doesn't just feel bad — population-level data shows anxiety and depression are associated with dying 7.9 years earlier. Voice biomarkers are involuntary neurological signals that bypass the cognitive filters causing people to minimize what they're experiencing. The signal is always there. In every conversation, every call, every meeting. Lucid monitors the biomarkers that predict these conditions before they become clinical.

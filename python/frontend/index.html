      Frequently Asked Questions

      Does this replace my therapist? ▼

      No. Lucid is a monitoring tool, not a treatment. Think of it like a heart rate monitor for your mental wellness — it tracks the signal continuously so you can spot trends early. It complements the care you already receive, whether that's therapy, medication, or self-care practices.

      Is this FDA-approved? ▼

      Lucid is a general wellness tool, not a medical device — similar to how Oura tracks HRV for wellness without being FDA-cleared as a diagnostic. The underlying voice biomarker model was clinically validated in a 14,898-participant study published in Annals of Family Medicine.

      71.3% accuracy — is that good enough? ▼

      The PHQ-9 — the gold-standard depression screening questionnaire used by clinicians — has comparable sensitivity for a single administration. The difference: the PHQ-9 is given once a year and relies on self-report. Lucid measures continuously, from involuntary signals, every time you speak. Continuous monitoring at 71% catches far more than annual screening at similar accuracy.

      Why doesn't the speech timer exactly match how long I talk? ▼

      Speaker verification checks every ~1.2 seconds to ensure only YOUR voice is being captured. This privacy-first approach means some speech at the boundaries of each segment isn't counted. The timer shows verified speech only — not all detected audio.

      Press ⌘+Shift+D to open the Speaker Verification panel and watch verification happening in real time. You can also go to Settings → Voice Profile and enhance your voice profile to improve matching accuracy.

      How does the analysis work? When does it trigger? ▼

      Once 30 seconds of verified speech accumulates, a grace period begins. Lucid keeps collecting up to 60 seconds, then auto-triggers the analysis. If you stop speaking between 30-60 seconds, the analysis auto-triggers after 15 seconds of silence.

      What is the Health Score? ▼

      A real-time wellness score from 0-100, derived from involuntary vocal biomarkers — signals your autonomic nervous system produces that you can't control or fake. Updated every time you speak. No questionnaires. No self-reporting.

      It combines seven voice dimensions — stress, wellbeing, calmness, activation, depression risk, anxiety risk, and emotional stability — weighted by validation strength, plus your calm-time recovery ratio.

      Where does my voice data go? ▼

      Nowhere. Literally. The full 244M-parameter model runs 100% on your Mac. Your voice is processed locally and never uploaded to any server. Lucid works with your internet disconnected — that's how you know.

      There are no cloud servers, no data collection, and no third parties. Your raw voice audio is never stored — only the derived wellness scores are kept in a local database.

      What are Rhythm Rings? ▼

      Three daily goals displayed as concentric rings: minutes of speech (Speak), minutes in the calm zone (Calm), and number of check-ins. Try to close all three rings each day — like Apple's Activity Rings but for vocal wellness.

      What is the Zone Bar? ▼

      A color-coded timeline showing your stress zones throughout the day. Green = Calm, Yellow = Steady, Orange = Tense, Red = Stressed. It gives you an at-a-glance view of how your day has unfolded emotionally.

      How does voice analysis detect well-being? ▼

      Lucid uses the Kintsugi DAM model — a 244M-parameter transformer trained on 35,000 diverse subjects. It was clinically validated in a 14,898-participant study published in Annals of Family Medicine (2025), achieving 71.3% sensitivity and 73.5% specificity from just 25 seconds of natural speech.

      What is calibration and why does it take a few days? ▼

      Lucid needs about 3 days of voice data to build your personal baseline. Everyone's voice is different — what sounds "stressed" for you might be "normal" for someone else. Calibration learns YOUR patterns so scores are personalized, not generic.

      Can Lucid hear other people's voices? ▼

      No. Lucid uses ECAPA-TDNN speaker verification to check every ~1.2-second audio segment against your enrolled voice profile. Segments that don't match you are rejected. This means coworkers on calls, TV audio, and other speakers are filtered out.

      What are Echoes and Compass? ▼

      Echoes are pattern discoveries — insights Lucid finds in your data over time (e.g., "Your stress peaks on Wednesdays"). They appear after 7+ days of use. Compass is your weekly wellness direction — ascending, holding, or descending — updated every Monday with a personal intention you can set.

      About the Science
      

            
      
                
      Voice biomarker technology — clinically validated, peer-reviewed, and backed by $30M in R&D — has existed for years. But it was only available through hospital integrations. Apple Silicon changed that.
      
            
      

            
      
                
      Clinical Validation
      
                
      14,898 participants in a double-blind study published in Annals of Family Medicine (January 2025). 71.3% sensitivity and 73.5% specificity from just 25 seconds of natural speech.
      
                
      For context: the PHQ-9 — the standard depression screening questionnaire — has comparable sensitivity for a single administration, but it's given once a year and relies entirely on self-report.
      
            
      

            
      
                
      The AI
      
                
      244M-parameter transformer (Whisper-Small architecture) trained on 35,000 diverse subjects — 110x larger than the next-best voice biomarker dataset. Backed by $30M in R&D from Kintsugi Health.
